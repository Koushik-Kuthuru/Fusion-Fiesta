ABOUT

Prototype Parade at a event bring fresh perspectives and creativity to the forefront, inspiring students to think outside the box. These ideas can range from unique project concepts to groundbreaking solutions in technology, sustainability, or art. By showcasing such innovations, the event encourages a culture of experimentation, problem-solving, and collaboration. It fosters an environment where students feel motivated to push boundaries and contribute to the advancement of knowledge, making the event a platform for future leaders and change-makers.

Details General Rules Contact Us
  • • Theme: Present an innovative idea focusing on creativity, technology, sustainability, social impact, or any relevant field. A theme may be provided if applicable.
  • • Eligibility: The competition is open to all college students. Participation can be individual or in teams (max 3 members per team).
  • • Registration: Participants must complete the online registration form or contact the organizing team by the given registration deadline.
  • • Presentation Time: Each participant/team will have a maximum of 5 minutes to present their idea.
  • • Q&A Session: A 2-3 minute Q&A session will follow after each presentation for judges and the audience to ask questions.
  • • Format of Presentation: Participants can present their ideas through PowerPoint, posters, prototypes, models, or videos.
  • • Submission Guidelines: If required, participants must submit a brief summary or abstract of their idea by the registration deadline.
  • • Materials Provided: Basic materials such as projectors, screens, and microphones will be provided. Participants must bring their own laptops, models, or additional props.
  • • Originality: All ideas must be original. Plagiarism or copying existing concepts will lead to disqualification.
  • • Presentation Limit: Presentations must be completed within 5 minutes. Exceeding the time limit may result in penalties.
  • • Clarity of Communication: The idea should be communicated in a clear, structured, and easy-to-understand manner.
  • • Visual Aids: Participants are encouraged to use visuals (e.g., models, slides, prototypes) to support their presentation.
  • • Feasibility: The idea should be realistic and feasible for implementation in real-world scenarios.
  • • Ethics: The idea must be ethical and should not promote illegal, harmful, or immoral activities.
  • • No Commercial Promotion: The competition is for innovative ideas and solutions, not for promoting existing products or services.
  • • Respect for Judges: Participants should maintain a professional attitude and show respect to the judges and other participants.
  • • Team Participation: Each team can have a maximum of 3 members. If participating as an individual, the participant must present alone.
  • • Adherence to Deadlines: All submissions, including the registration and preliminary abstracts, must be submitted on time. Late submissions will not be considered.
